The dissolve from stressed to calm woman feels gendered and patronizing. And 'Good Different' tells me nothing about why I should shop there for my family.

This concept misreads me entirely. I'm not overwhelmed by deals—I'm energized by getting value for my family. Comparing prices is adulting, not chaos. The visual of a stressed woman being 'rescued' by simpler shopping feels like it's playing to outdated stereotypes about women and decision-making. What I actually want to know: Is your produce fresh? Are your products genuinely better value or just presented more simply? Do you have organic options my kids will actually eat? The 'confidence' claim is empty without substance. I don't need fewer choices; I need better information. This feels like it's selling simplicity to people they assume can't handle complexity, which is insulting. My friends and I are sophisticated shoppers who read labels, compare unit prices, and make intentional choices. We're not looking for someone to dumb down our shopping experience.

Works: The recognition that grocery shopping can be stressful is real

Concerns: Patronizing portrayal of shoppers as overwhelmed and incapable; No actual information about product quality, value, or family-friendly options; 'Good Different' is meaningless without substance

Would convince: Show me actual product quality, ethical sourcing, and real value comparisons. Testimonials from other parents about specific products their kids love. Stop treating shoppers like we need saving and start treating us like intelligent consumers who want transparency.
