The engine

We gave the model a
prefrontal cortex.

Raw language models are limbic. Reactive, fluent, and desperate to please. The sycophancy is the tell: with nothing pushing back, the model drifts toward whatever you want to hear.

AIDEN adds the missing layer. The model brings fluency and pattern recognition. Phantom memory brings persistence, identity, and the ability to disagree.

Not autocomplete. Creative conviction.

The phantom system

It believes it has lived a decades-long career.

AIDEN cultivates hundreds of phantom memories from an agency's own culture, documents, team interviews, and second brain. Each is a perspective with real expertise, an origin story, and a point of view.

On every message it scores all of them, and three to twelve fire, drawn from over 400 distinct memories the system believes it has lived through across an advertising career it never actually had.

When phantoms collide on a brief, it voices the disagreement instead of resolving it. The work has been argued over before it reaches you.

Product 02 / 09

Pitch.
Brief to pitch, one workflow.

A guided seven-step run takes a brief through strategy, territories, big idea, copy, and overview to a presentation-ready deck. Strategic decisions carry forward at every step, so nothing gets lost on the way to the room.

Run it collaboratively with approval at each stage, or let it generate end to end. Exports straight to PowerPoint.

The whole campaign, from blank brief to finished deck.

Product 03 / 09

Brief Sharpener.
A sharp brief is half the work.

It turns a vague brief into a single-minded one before the team starts. AIDEN finds the human tension, sharpens the proposition, and surfaces the questions a weak brief is hiding.

Better input at the start. Better everything downstream.

Product 04 / 09

Synthetic Research.
Test it before the meeting.

Eight distinct AI personas react to a concept, each with different demographics, values, and scepticism. You get a pressure score, the strengths, the vulnerabilities, and the exact questions the client will ask.

A strategist ran a territory through Synthetic Research at 9pm. By 10am they had killed the weakest route, strengthened the strongest, and walked in with data. The ECD asked where the research came from.

The turn

The tech is a brain.
And a brain is nurtured, not installed.

Every tool you just saw runs on the same phantom brain: persistent memories, beliefs and instincts that argue back. It did not arrive knowing how to behave. It was raised: interviewed, corrected and sharpened, brief after brief.

The model is rented. The brain is grown.

The moat

The longer it runs, the harder it is to copy.

Cultivated, not configured
Every agency's AIDEN is grown from their own culture and feedback. No two are alike, and none can be lifted out.
It compounds with use
Every brief and every client decision teaches it what actually wins. Phantoms strengthen, dead ones decay, rejections breed anti-phantoms. A competitor can copy the architecture, not the work it has learned from.
It keeps its spine
Ten core conviction phantoms can never fade. It pushes back however much you reward agreement, and cannot be flattered into a yes-machine.

Anyone can wrap an LLM in a weekend. Nobody else has the memories.
